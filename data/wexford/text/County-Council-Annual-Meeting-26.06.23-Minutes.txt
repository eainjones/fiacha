Opening Remarks – An Cathaoirleach 

Outgoing Cathaoirleach Cllr. G. Lawlor opened the 126th Annual Meeting of Wexford 
County Council and welcomed the attendance. Cllr. Lawlor welcomed Minister James 
Browne, Senator Malcolm Byrne, former Minister John Browne and Superintendent James

Doyle. Cllr. Lawlor also welcomed Cllr. J. Fleming’s sons Jack and Michael, his extended 
family, his fiancé Judy Mellon and Cllr. Fleming’s loyal supporters.  

Cllr. Lawlor remarked on the many events that took place during the year and said he felt 
proud and privileged to have had the opportunity to represent his county. Cllr. Lawlor 
acknowledged many important events and projects progressed, such as: 

•  CPO of land for SETU campus, 
•  Securing Fleadh Cheoil na hÉireann, 
•  Turning the sod on the Women’s Refuge, 
•  Welcoming the Rose tour, 
•  Strengthening of Georgia Southern University relationship and signing of contract 

for works on the adoration convent, 

•  Enniscorthy Technology Park, 
•  Esmonde St. development, 
•  New fire station in New Ross, 
•  High Hill Norman Park,  
•  Emigrant Park, New Ross, 
•  New boxing Club in Ferndale, 
•  Wexford Rape Crisis Centre, 
•  Trinity Wharf 

Cllr. G. Lawlor thanked Mr. T. Enright and all the staff for their support, and he 
acknowledged the contribution of Leas Cathaoirleach Cllr. M. Whelan. Cllr. Lawlor 
congratulated his party colleague Cllr. M. Bell on her year as mayor and thanked his fellow 
Councillors, and his family for their support. 

 All Members joined in congratulating the outgoing Cathaoirleach for his very busy year 
and spoke of his outstanding performance during his term of office in representing 
Wexford County Council and County Wexford with pride, professionalism, and 
commitment. 

Chief Executive Tom Enright joined the Elected Members in congratulating the outgoing 
Cathaoirleach on his professional and exceptional performance during his year in office. 
Mr. Enright praised Cllr. Lawlor for representing the Council nationally and internationally 
with distinction.

On a vote of 22 in favour of Cllr. J. Fleming and 10 votes in favour of Cllr. B. Murphy, Cllr. G. 
Lawlor declared Cllr. J. Fleming to be elected Cathaoirleach of Wexford County Council for 
the 2023-2024 term.   

Cllr. J. Fleming was presented by Cllr. G. Lawlor with the Chain of Office, following which 
Cllr. J. Fleming assumed the Chair. 

Cathaoirleach J. Fleming acknowledged the presence of Minister James Browne, Senator 
Malcolm Byrne, former Minister John Browne and Superintendent James Doyle. Cllr. 
Fleming thanked his proposer and seconder, commiserated with Cllr. B. Murphy and 
congratulated Cllr. G. Lawlor on his exemplary performance during his term of office. 

Cllr. Fleming promised to represent the people of Wexford with passion and pride, as he 
did in his sporting days. Cllr. Fleming acknowledged the great work done over recent years 
with all areas of the County benefitting. He highlighted a number of issues that he believes 
will be a matter of utmost importance in the year ahead, including:  

• 
• 
• 
• 
• 
• 

Housing supply increased 
Vacant homes and dereliction to be tackled 
Delivery of serviced sites in towns and villages 
Construction of an advance factory in New Ross 
Awareness of climate change and use of best practices 
Secure tier 1 status for Rosslare Europort 

Cllr. Fleming acknowledged his family who were present especially his sister Anne who is 
home from Australia, his sons Michael and Jack, and his fiancé Judy. He noted how proud 
his deceased parents Kitty and Paddy would be. Cllr. Fleming thanked his canvassers 
present, Mike, Seamus, Johnny, and Martin. 

The Members and Chief Executive joined in congratulating Cllr. Fleming on his election to 
the office of Cathaoirleach and wished him every success in the year ahead.
