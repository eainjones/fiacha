At  the  outset  the  Cathaoirleach  welcomed  Members  to  this  special  meeting  to  receive 
presentations  from  prospective  candidates  seeking  the  nomination  of  Wexford  County 
Council  in  the  forthcoming  Presidential  Election.    The  Cathaoirleach  invited  the  County 
Secretary  to  advise  Members  of  how  the  special  meeting  would  be  conducted  and  to 
agree a proposed procedure for voting on 10th September.  

Members were advised that the agenda for the meeting on 10th September would be going 
out this afternoon and that any nominations would need to be submitted in writing by  
2.30p.m.  today.  The  County  Secretary  notified  the  meeting  that  Cllr.  Ger  Carthy  had 
already  proposed  John  Gallagher  for  the  nomination.  He  also  advised  that  Gavin  Duffy 
would not be attending the today’s meeting. 

The County Secretary then explained the method by which the voting would take place on 
10th  September  as  was  set  out  in  the  document  circulated  to  the  Members  and  invited 
questions from Members with regard to the procedure.  It was agreed that the nomination 
of  a  person  to  be  candidate  at  the  Presidential  Election  would  be  on  the  agenda  for 
Monday 10th September 2018 and on the proposal of Cllr. T. Dempsey seconded by Cllr. 
M. Byrne the procedure as circulated was agreed. 

The Cathaoirleach then called on the first candidate Senator Joan Freeman to address the 
Council.    Senator  Freeman  in  her  address  to  the  Members  outlined  why  she  intended 
running for the Office of President, summarised her achievements and sought the support 
of the Local Authority in order to allow her to participate in the Presidential Election.

In  particular,  she  highlighted  her  role  in  setting  up  Pieta  House  and  highlighted  the 
characteristics  she  would  bring  to  the  Presidency  -  leadership,  resilience  and  ability  to 
persuade and influence to highlight mental health and other issues.  

Following a number of questions from the Members on the content of her address Senator 
Freeman then left the meeting having being thanked by the Cathaoirleach for attending. 

The  next  candidate  was  Sean  Gallagher  and  he  then  addressed  the  Council.    To  begin 
with he gave his condolences to the Cathaoirleach for his recent loss. He advised that he 
was  seeking  the  nomination  from  Wexford  County  Council  and  if  so  honoured  by  the 
Council to be nominated as their candidate, he would ensure that he would focus on all of 
those on the margins of society. 

He then set out his vision of the role of the President.  

In  particular,  he  mentioned  the  Disability  Groups  and  visiting  Ireland’s  peace  keeping 
forces abroad.  He also gave an explanation of his previous campaign for the presidency 
in 2011 and in particular the Frontline debate which took place on RTE at that time and his 
subsequent dealings with RTE over the years since that broadcast..  

He  outlined  his activities  in  business,  entrepreneurship,  community,  journalism and other 
areas since the last election. He also answered questions from Members on a wide range 
of topics. 

The Cathaoirleach thanked Mr. Gallagher for attending and presenting to the meeting. 

Sarah Louise Mulligan was then asked to address the Council.  She focused on a number 
of issues, child abuse, elder abuse, suicide, her pro-life stance, entrepreneurship and her 
support for President Trump.  She hoped to be a voice for the voicelesss in Irish Society. 

Following  her  presentation  Sarah  Louise  Mulligan  responded  to  a  number  of  questions 
raised  by  the  Members  and  then  left  the  meeting  having  been  thanked  by  the 
Cathaoirleach for attending. 

This concluded the business of the meeting.
