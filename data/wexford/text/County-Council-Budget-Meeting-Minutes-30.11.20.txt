1.  Chief Executive Introduction 

Mr. Tom Enright CE introduced the Budget to the Council outlining the difficulties 
encountered in delivering a budget for 2021 in light of the significant financial impact 
and challenges created by the covid pandemic and associated public health measures. 
He thanked the Elected Members for their continued support during the COVID-19 
pandemic and spoke about the many challenges facing County Wexford in 2021 and 
beyond as a result.  

Mr. Enright assured the Members that the Budget as presented supports the continuity 
of services in the County for 2021 and that it is important to protect local services for 
the County and its citizens as we emerge from Covid 19 restrictions and challenges. 

2.  Presentation by Head of Finance 

Ms. A. O’Neill presented an overview of the Draft Budget for 2021 and outlined some of 
the  factors  and  considerations  that  have  informed  Wexford  County  Council’s  draft 
Budget for 2021. These include: 

  The exceptional and unprecedented financial impact of 2020. 
  Ongoing uncertainties in relation to the track of the pandemic and its continuing 

impact on Economic activity.  

  Ongoing covid impact on expenditure and income of the Council. 

She also noted that: 
  Central funding remains at 2013 levels. 
  Roads and Grants are still below 2008 levels. 
  LPT revaluation /review had been postponed again. 
 
Increased costs across a number of heading.  
  Limited buoyancy in LPT and rates income for 2021. 

She  advised  members  that  the  following  proposals  were  included  in  the  Draft 
Budget: 

  General Municipal Allocations (GMA) retained at 2020 levels; 
  Annual Rate On Valuation (ARV) retained at 2020 level (€0.246); 
  Rates relief per Vacant Premises to be retained at 90% subject to the terms and 

conditions of the scheme; 

  Rates Incentive schemes to be adjusted to:  

a) Redistribute RIS funding to SME sector. 

  b) Move the remaining 83 New Ross TC accounts another stage back to the     
      general RIS scheme.
