At the outset of the meeting, the Chief Executive Mr. Enright addressed the meeting and advised 
the Members that on Friday 12 October 2021, he would attend a hearing of the Standards in Public 
Office Commission. The hearing was being convened to consider allegations against Mr. Enright that 
he had breached the Code of Conduct for Employees. 

Mr. Enright advised the Members that he was unable to offer further comment, other than to say 
he would robustly defend himself in respect of the allegations and would at the earliest opportunity 
bring a full report to the Council on the matter.

Vacant Homes Report 

Ms. Carolyne Godkin, A/Director of Services presented the Report and responded to the 
Members’ queries. 

It was noted that the Council expects to appoint a full-time Vacant Homes Officer in Qtr. 1 
2022, while it is possible that the issue of vacant homes will also considered at regional 
level, with the possibility of funding being made available regionally to address this 
initiative. 

5.  

Section 85 Agreement - Kildare and Wexford County Council Agreement  
– O’Hanrahan Bridge, New Ross, Co. Wexford 

On the proposal of Cllr. M. Whelan, seconded by Cllr. A. Connick, the Council unanimously 
resolved to enter into an agreement with Kilkenny County Council under Section 85 of the 
Local Government Act 2001 in respect of the repair and improvement of O’Hanrahan 
Bridge, New Ross. 

Mr. E. Hore, Director of Services advised the Members that in the course of the proposed 
work, it may be possible to widen the existing footpath on one side of the bridge, thereby 
facilitating the linking of New Ross town to the Southeast Greenway, which is currently 
under construction.  

Mr. Hore anticipated that the design of the proposed improvement works would take 
approximately two years to complete, and that a road closure may be required for a period 
of time to facilitate some of the construction works.
