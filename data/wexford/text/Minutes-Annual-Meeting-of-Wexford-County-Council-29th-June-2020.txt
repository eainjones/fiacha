The  Cathaoirleach  called  the  meeting  to  order  and  welcomed  the  attendance,  including 
family members and distinguished guests of those present. 

Cllr. Sheehan paid tribute to his Councillor colleagues and thanked them for their support 
to  him  during  his  term  as  Cathaoirleach  of  the  Council.  He  also  thanked  the  Chief 
Executive, members of the Management Team and all staff of Wexford County Council for 
their courtesy, professionalism and public service during the preceding year. 

Cllr Sheehan also thanked the public for its patience and  sacrifices during the COVID-19 
crisis and assured them that better times were ahead as progress continued to be made 
combatting the virus. 

The  Members  and  Executive  joined  in  congratulating  Cllr  Sheehan  on  his  performance 
over  the  past  year  with  many  highlighting  the  outgoing  Cathaoirleach’s  impressive  lst  of 
achievements during his term of office.

On a vote of 22 votes in favour of Cllr. Carthy and 9 votes in favour of Cllr. Walsh, 
Cllr.  G.  Carthy  was  declared  elected  Cathaoirleach  of Wexford  County  Council  for 
the 2020 - 2021 term of office. Cllr. Carthy took the Chair. 

The Cathaoirleach thanked his proposer and seconder and commiserated with his 
colleague  Cllr.  O.  Walsh.  He  stated  that  among  his  priorities  for  the  coming  year 
would  be  the  seeking  of  investment  in  Rosslare  Europort  and  the  support  of  rural 
communities throughout Wexford.  

Cllr. Carthy thanked his family for their unwavering support  throughout his political 
life and for accepting the accompanying daily personal sacrifices that were involved. 
He also paid tribute to his late father and former Cathaoirleach of Wexford County 
Council,  Leo  Carthy,  who  instilled  a  keen  sense  of  civic  duty  and  pride  in  Cllr. 
Carthy from an early age.
