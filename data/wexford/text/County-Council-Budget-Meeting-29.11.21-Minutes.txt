1.  Chief Executive Introduction 

Mr. Tom Enright Chief Executive introduced the draft 2022 Budget to the Council. He 
thanked the Elected Members for their continued support during the COVID-19 
pandemic and outlined the many challenges facing County Wexford in 2022. 

Mr. Enright assured the Members that the Council 2022 Budget and the services 
provided therein offered the way forward for the County as it emerges from Covid 19 
restrictions and addresses the statutory roles and responsibilities of the local authority. 

2.  Presentation by Head of Finance 

Ms. A. O’Neill presented the Draft Budget 2022 and outlined some of the circumstances 
and arrangements which have framed the draft Budget. These include: 

•  No central funding increase forecast in 2022 
•  Road grants continue below 2008 level 
•  Challenges  associated  with  collection  of  Commercial  Rates  and  possible 

appeals in respect of rate accounts  
Income uncertainties as result of Covid 19 

• 
•  Proposal to retain Relief on Vacant premises at the 2021 level 
•  Proposal to retain GMA’s at 2021 level 
•  Proposal to retain Annual Rate on Valuation at 2021 level 
•  Decision to retain LPT at 2021 level 
•  Continuation of Rates Incentive Schemes 

Ms. O’Neill then introduced each of the Budget Tables and together with her colleagues 
from the Management Team responded to queries  from the Members on a number of 
elements of the Draft Budget as the Members considered the draft Budget in detail. 

•  Housing & Building: 

Maintenance costs – including Planned maintenance 
Homeless arrangements 
Maintenance of lanes within local authority estates 

•  Roads 

Local Road Maintenance 
School Warden Service 
Road drainage costs 

•  Planning / Development Tourist Development & Promotion 

Duncannon Fort 
Consideration of a County Museum 

•  Environment Services  Slipway charges 

Charges to Chartered Boats 

The meeting adjourned at this point to allow a comfort break and to allow the room to 
be properly ventilated in accordance with public health advice.

On  resumption  of  the  meeting,  and  following  further  consideration  of  the  allocations 
within the draft budget, the Cathaoirleach put the following decisions before the Council 
for consideration: 

(a) Decision  of  percentage  of  commercial  rates  relief  to  be  offered  to  vacant 

premises 

On  the  proposal  of  Cllr.  G.  Lawlor,  seconded  by  Cllr.  A.  Connick  and  agreed 
unanimously,  the  Council  resolved  to  apply  commercial  rates  relief  on  vacant 
premises at the rate of 90% 

(b) Decision on Draft Budget 

On  the  proposal  of  Cllr.  P.  Breen,  seconded  by  J.  O’Rourke  and  agreed 
unanimously,  the  Council  resolved  to  adopt  the  draft  Budget  2022  without 
amendment. 

(c) Decision regarding Annual Rate on Valuation 

On the proposal of Cllr. D. Devereux, seconded by Cllr. J. Fleming and agreed 
unanimously,  the  Council  resolved  to  determine  at  0.246  the  annual  rate  on 
valuation to be levied in accordance with the local authority budget as adopted 
above. 

In  addition  to  the  above,  and  on  the  proposal  of  Cllr.  J.  Fleming  seconded  by  Cllr.  M. 
Whelan and agreed unanimously, the Council also agreed implement the Rates Incentive 
Scheme  and  Special Rates  Incentive Scheme  in  accordance  with  the  criteria  set  out  by 
the Head of Finance. 

THIS CONCLUDED THE BUSINESS OF THE MEETING.
