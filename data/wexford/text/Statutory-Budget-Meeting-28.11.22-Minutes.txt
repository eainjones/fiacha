1. 

Opening address – Mr. Tom Enright, Chief Executive 

Mr. T. Enright advised that the 2023 budget was a particularly challenging budget to draft. 
Soaring energy costs and rising inflation are just some of the factors to be considered. Mr. 
T. Enright outlined the ambitious economic and special project programme being 
delivered. He also highlighted the additional funding the draft budget will provide in the 
Municipal Districts, in addition to a provision for funding Fleadh Cheoil na hEireann, should 
Wexford be successful in its bid. 

2. 

Draft Budget Overview – Presentation 

Ms. A. O’Neill gave a presentation which outlined the following: 

•  Backdrop to budget 2023. 
•  Budget gap challenges for 2023. 
•  Funding position for 2023. 
•  Budget 2023 income and expenditure summary. 
•  Expenditure and income movement overview. 
•  2023 Budget decisions to be made, including: 

o  Local Property Tax (LPT), and local adjustment factor  
o  Commercial Rates and ARV for 2023,  
o 
o  Rates on Vacant Properties  

Introduction of Small Business Support Grant (SBSG)  

Ms. A. O’Neill responded to a wide range of queries from the Elected Members in regard to 
the following: 

Implications of the 2019 Rates Revaluation Appeals outcome for the Council. 

•  Tiered vacancy bands and rates. 
• 
•  What do we do with Fleadh funding provision if we are unsuccessful? 
•  Any requirement on Valuation Tribunal to respond to appeals in a timely manner? 
•  Potential for receiving rates from solar farms. 
•  How much from our own resources goes towards pension, pay & FEMPI? 
•  Confirmation that funding is available for Enniscorthy Town Regeneration Plan. 
•  Confirmation that if Fleadh bid is successful, Council won’t seek a further rate 

increase next year. 

•  Need for rates increase as businesses emerge from Covid. 
•  Request that additional income for Districts be distributed pro rata based on the 

number of Councillors in the District.

Decision on Rates to be applied to Vacant Premises 

Recommendation for Change from 2023  
•  Retain 90% relief for all properties with annual rates to €2,000 
•  Apply 80% relief for properties with annual rates over €2,000 and less than €10,000 
•  Apply 60% relief for properties with annual rates over €10,000 

On the proposal of Cllr. Diarmuid Devereux, seconded by Cllr. Willie Kavanagh, the Council 
resolved to adopt the recommendation for change to rates to be applied to vacant 
properties. 

Adoption of Annual Rate on Valuation (ARV) 

Recommendation – Adopt €0.253 an increase of 3% in the ARV over 2022 level 

Cllr. Michael Sheehan proposed to increase the Amenity Grants from €6,000 per Member 
to €7,000 per Member with the consequent reduction being met from the Community 
budget, this was seconded by Cllr. Cathal Byrne and agreed by all. 

Cllr. Cathal Byrne proposed the abolition of slipway charges, this was seconded by Cllr. Pat 
Barden. There was a lengthy discussion during which Cllr.’s D. Devereux, J. Moore and P. 
Breen spoke against the abolition of slipway charges. In addition to the proposers, Cllr.’s J. 
Owens and M. Whelan spoke in favour of the abolition of slipway charges. 

The Cathaoirleach advised that there would be a roll call vote to establish if slipway 
charges would be abolished. The outcome was that the proposal to abolish slipway charges 
was defeated with 6 in favour and 22 against. The voting was as follows:
