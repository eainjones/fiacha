Votes of Sympathy: 

The Cathaoirleach offered the sympathy of the Council to the family and friends of the late Vicky 
Phelan.  The  Cathaoirleach  described  Vicky  Phelan  as  an  articulate,  fearless,  compassionate  and 
inspirational lady. He said she selflessly fought on behalf of others in spite of her own battle. He 
acknowledged her support for the kit out the refuge campaign in Wexford. The Cathaoirleach noted 
the huge loss, in particular to the women of Ireland and he advised that a book of condolence would 
be opened in County Hall and each of the Municipal Districts. 

The attendance stood for a minutes silence in memory of the deceased. 

Cllr. B.A. Murphy said there isn’t a person in the Chamber  whose family hasn’t been affected by 
cancer and offered her sincere condolence to Vicky’s children and family. 

Cllr. B. Murphy echoed the sentiments of the previous speakers and expressed huge admiration for 
how Vicky had dedicated the last years of her life. 

Cllr. D. Hynes agreed with the sentiments expressed and said it is important that words are followed 
with actions. 

Cllr. J. Owens said he felt like he knew her even though he never met her and expressed his deepest 
sympathy. 

Mr.  T.  Enright  expressed  deepest  sympathy  on  behalf  of  the  management  and  staff  of  Wexford 
County Council. 

1. 

Presentation by Housing Staff 

Mr. T. Enright welcomed the opportunity to have a special meeting outlining the work that 
is ongoing and he outlined the Council’s ambitions for social and affordable housing in the 
County.

•  Structure of Housing and new roles 
•  Demand figures by District and distribution of demand by unit type 
•  HSSU structure, role and stats 
•  Grants stats 
•  Traveller accommodation programme 
•  Housing allocation stats and draft housing allocation scheme 
•  Choice based letting and housing stock stats 
•  Response & planned maintenance and energy retrofit programme
