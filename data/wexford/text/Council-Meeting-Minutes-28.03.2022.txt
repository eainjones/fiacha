4.  Wexford  County  Development  Plan Review 

4.1   Strategic Environmental Assessment and Natura Impact Report on 
the Draft Plan and Proposed Material Alterations to the County 
Development Plan. 

Before the decision on proceeding to the next stage of the Development 
Plan was discussed two notices of motions were put to the floor as 
follows: 

           4.2  

 Notices of Motion  

4.2.1 

It was proposed by Cllr J Fleming -  

To amend Section 5.7 of Volume 10 Energy Strategy contained in 
the Draft Plan to require that Wind turbines are located a minimum 
of 400m from an adjoining landholding and a minimum of 6 times 
the tip height of the wind turbine or 900m (whichever is the greater) 
from a residential property.  
(Relevant submission WXF  C3-164) 

Reason:  

To minimise impacts on adjoining residences and 
properties in the interests of the proper planning and 
development  of the area. 

Following discussion on this matter and advice from the Senior 

3. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
      
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Planner, Cllr J. Fleming withdrew this Motion. 

4.2.2   It was proposed by Cllr J Fleming and seconded by Cllr C Byrne -  

To not add the Farmhouse at the Mayne, Enniscorthy (RPS 
Reference 15702006) to the Record of Protected Structures. 
(Relevant submission WXF-C- 31). 

Reason:   The protected status of the house is not compatible 
with the zoning of the surrounding lands. 

Following discussion on this matter and advice from the Senior 
Planner Cllr J. Fleming withdrew this motion. 

At this point in the meeting Mr. D Houston advised the members that the specialist 
consultants engaged to carry out environmental assessments of the Draft Plan had 
completed their assessments and that these were circulated for the Members 
consideration in advance of the meeting.  

Mr. Houston drew the Members attention to the conclusions of the assessments 
and stated that the proposed material alterations and the associated assessments 
could now proceed to the next stage and be placed on public display.  

On the proposal of Cllr P Breen and seconded by Cllr Oliver Walsh the Council 
unanimously resolved to proceed to the next stage of the development plan 
process.

8.  Chief Executive's Report 

The Chief Executive updated the members on the work that was being carried out 
nationally and locally, in particular at Rosslare Harbour, in response to the 
Ukrainian Displaced Persons crisis.  

Mr. Enright further advised that it has been agreed nationally that the Community 
Response Forum (chaired by the Chief Executive of the Local Authority and 
including the membership of the Cathaoirleach) would be reconvened locally to 
coordinate the response of all relevant agency to this crisis. The support of the 
volunteers, agencies and the people of Rosslare in dealing with the situation to 
date.was commended by a number of the Members

ThIs being the final meeting to be attended by retiring Director of Service Mr. 
Tony Larkin, an Cathaoirleach congratulated Mr. Tony Larkin on his 42 years

retirement and on his exemplary service to Wexford County Council and 
outlined some of the many achievements that Mr. Larkin was involved in 
during his 42 years’ service.
