Cathaoirleach Opening Remarks 

Outgoing Cathaoirleach Cllr. P. Breen opened the Annual Meeting of Wexford County 
Council and welcomed the attendance. Cllr. Breen welcomed Deputy Malcolm Byrne, 
Ceann Comhairle Verona Murphy and those in the public gallery. 

Cllr. P. Breen thanked Mr. E. Taaffe and all the staff for their support, and he acknowledged 
the contribution of Leas Cathaoirleach Cllr. G. Carthy. Cllr. Breen thanked his fellow 
Councillors for their support. 

The Members joined in congratulating the outgoing Cathaoirleach representing Wexford 
County Council and County Wexford with pride, professionalism, and commitment.  

Chief Executive Mr. E. Taaffe joined the Elected Members in congratulating the outgoing 
Cathaoirleach on his professional performance during his year in office. Mr. Taaffe praised 
Cllr. Breen’s calm and courteous manner and for representing the Council nationally and 
internationally with distinction, acknowledging his outstanding role in leading the Wexford 
delegation to Savannah. 

1.  

Election of Cathaoirleach 

Cllr. Joe Sullivan was proposed by Cllr. B.A. Murphy and his nomination was seconded by 
Cllr. O. Walsh. As there were no other nominations, Cathaoirleach Cllr. P. Breen declared 
Cllr. Joe Sullivan elected to the position of Cathaoirleach. 

Cllr. J. Sullivan was presented by Cllr. P. Breen with the Chain of Office, following which Cllr. 
J. Sullivan assumed the Chair. 

Cllr. Sullivan’s first act as Cathaoirleach was to present outgoing Cathaoirleach Cllr. P. 
Breen with a certificate and medal acknowledging his service. 

Cllr. J. Sullivan called for a minute’s silence which the chamber observed to mark the 
passing of Mrs. Ann Brennan, mother of Deputy Brian Brennan. 

Cllr. Sullivan thanked his proposer and seconder and acknowledged what a huge honour it 
is to be elected Cathaoirleach. Cllr. Sullivan thanked Mr. Lorcan Allen and the late Eileen 
Higgins for giving him an opportunity in politics, and his long-time friend Des Carthy. He 
also thanked his wife Patricia, and sons Cian and Tom for their help and support. 

Cllr. J. Sullivan said he is looking forward to the return of Fleadh Cheoil na hÉireann and 
emphasised the importance of progressing the SETU campus in Wexford, the completion of 
the M11 – M25 motorway and the development of the St. Waleran’s site. 

Councillor’s G. Carthy, J. Owens, D. Kenny, J. Fleming, P. Breen, G. Laffan, B.A. Murphy, J. 
Codd, N. Boland, P. Kehoe, P. Barden and A. Donohoe congratulated Cllr. Sullivan on his 
election. Chief Executive Mr. E. Taaffe welcomed Deputy Malcolm Byrne, Ceann Comhairle 
Verona Murphy and Cllr. Sullivan’s family Trish, Cian, and Tom. Mr. Taaffe congratulated

2. 

Election of Leas-Cathaoirleach 

Cllr. Oliver Walsh was proposed by Cllr. A. Donohoe and his nomination was seconded by. 
Cllr. P. Breen. 

As there were no other nominations Cathaoirleach Cllr. J. Sullivan declared Cllr. O. Walsh 
elected to the position of Leas Cathaoirleach. Cllr. O. Walsh was presented by Cllr. J. 
Sullivan with the Chain of Office. 

Cllr. O. Walsh thanked his proposer and seconder and congratulated Cllr. Sullivan and his 
family on his election.  

Councillor’s J. Codd, J. Owens, B.A. Murphy, M. Sheehan, C. Walsh, P. Barden, and P. Kehoe 
congratulated Cllr. Walsh on his election. 

3. 

Schedule of Council Meetings 

On the proposal of Cllr. B.A. Murphy, seconded by Cllr. M. Sheehan the schedule of Council 
meetings was adopted.
