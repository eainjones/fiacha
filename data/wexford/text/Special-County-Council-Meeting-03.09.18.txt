At the outset the  Leas Cathaoirleach, Councillor Willie Fitzharris welcomed the Members 
to the Special Meeting to pass a formal vote of sympathy to the Cathaoirleach of Wexford 
County Council, Councillor Keith Doyle on the death of his mother Helen (Eileen) Doyle. 

Cllr. Fitzharris advised the Members that the Cathaoirleach had expressed his thanks and 
appreciation to the Members for convening the Special Meeting. 

The  Leas  Cathaoirleach  then  proposed  a  formal  vote  of  sympathy  to  the  Cathaoirleach 
and the Members of his family on the death of their mother Helen (Eileen) Doyle.  This was 
seconded by Cllr. R. Ireton.   

The  Leas  Cathaoirleach  then  invited  Cllr.  W.  Kavanagh  to  make  a  similar  proposal  on 
behalf of Enniscorthy Municipal District.  Cllr. W. Kavanagh then formally proposed a vote 
of  sympathy  to  the  Cathaoirleach  and  his  family  on  the  death  of  their  mother;  this  was 
seconded by Cllr. P. Kavanagh. 

Cllr. W.  Fitzharris  then  proposed  a  vote  of  sympathy  to  the  family  of  Cllr.  O. Walsh  who 
suffered a bereavement recently.   

Cllr. G. Lawlor then extended a vote of sympathy on behalf of the Labour Party.

Cllr. P. Kavanagh on behalf of Fine Gael extended a vote of sympathy to the Doyle Family 
and  noted  that  Mrs.  Doyle  had  travelled  extensively  during  her  life;  she  was  an 
independent woman and was actively involved in the Strawberry Fair Committee and had 
taken part in the business life of Enniscorthy Town.  She was a great character and added 
a lot to Enniscorthy politically, culturally and in business. 

Cllr. T. Walsh then extended his sympathy on behalf of People Before Profit.   

Cllr. J. Mythen extended his sympathy on behalf of Sinn Fein and advised that Mrs. Doyle 
was well liked in Enniscorthy, was very well respected and worked well with the people of 
the town.  

Cllr. D. Hynes extended his sympathy to the Doyle family. 

Cllr. J. Fleming as Cathaoirleach of New Ross Municipal District extended his sympathy to 
the Doyle Family.   

Mr. T.  Enright,  Chief Executive,  on  his own  behalf  and  on  behalf of  the  Staff  of Wexford 
County Council extended a vote of sympathy to the Doyle family. He referred to the recent 
celebration for former Cathaoirligh of Wexford County Council.  

Mrs. Doyle’s husband, Andy, was honoured at the ceremony for his time as Cathaoirleach 
of  Wexford  County  Council  and  she  sent  a  lovely  note  of  thanks  to  Mr.  D.  Minogue, 
Communications Officer which was much appreciated. 

A minutes silence was then called by the Leas Cathaoirleach. 

This concluded the meeting.
