Presentation by Housing Staff 

The Cathaoirleach opened the meeting and welcomed the attendance, including members of the 
media  and  the  public,  before  handing  over  to  Mr.  N.  McCabe,  A/SEO  Housing  to  make  a 
PowerPoint presentation to the meeting. 

Following the PowerPoint presentation, Chief Executive Tom Enright summarised the presentation 
and  also  identified  to  the  Members  some  of  the  challenges  involved  in  the  provision  of 
appropriate accommodation to homeless persons. 

Members Questions: 

There followed a wide-ranging and detailed discussion regarding the provision of housing services 
by  Wexford  County Council.  During  this  debate,  Members  raised  a  large  range  of  questions  and 
suggestions including the following:

development

through Repair / Renew Scheme 

  Provision of additional social housing in New Ross town 

  Funding and completion of outstanding housing adaption grant-aided works 

  Circulation on monthly basis of statistics regarding maintenance works sought / completed 

  Standard of repairs achieved when pre-letting works are being carried out 

  Addition of further contractors to the panel of approved housing repair contractors 

  Use of choice-based letting to speed up allocation process 

  Procedure to be followed when seeking homeless accommodation 

  Master Plan for St. Waleran’s Gorey 

  Development of Affordable Housing in Gorey and Enniscorthy 

  Extent of Council’s reliance on HAP and Leased properties to meet housing need 

  Clarification of number on housing list 

  Request for increase in no. of void properties being returned to letting standard 

  Completion of housing development in Bridgetown

Members of the Housing Team responded to the queries raised above. 

On  the  proposal  of  Cllr.  McDonald,  seconded  by  Cllr.  D.  Hynes,  it  was  agreed  that  the  Council 
would document a step-by-step process for the management of homeless presentations. 

It was further agreed that the Minister for Housing, Local Government and Heritage Mr. Daragh 
O’Brien  TD  would  be  invited  to  attend  a  meeting  of  Wexford  County  Council  at  the  earliest 
opportunity to respond to some of the issues raised above.
