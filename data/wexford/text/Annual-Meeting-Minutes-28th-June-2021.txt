The  Cathaoirleach  called  the  meeting  to  order  and  welcomed  the  attendance,  including 
family members and distinguished guests of those present. 

Cllr. Carthy paid tribute to his Councillor colleagues and thanked them for their support to 
him during his term as Cathaoirleach of the Council. He also thanked the Chief Executive, 
members  of  the  Management  Team  and  all  staff  of  Wexford  County  Council  for  their 
courtesy, professionalism and public service during the preceding year. 

The  Cathaoirleach  acknowledged  the  work of  Wexford  County  Council  in  addressing  the 
challenges  of  COVID-19  crisis  and  also  paid  tribute  to  the  public  for  their  support  of  the 
public health measures put in place to manage the Pandemic.  

The Cathaoirleach also thanked the outgoing Leas Cathaoirleach Cllr. Garry Laffan for his 
support to the Cathaoirleach throughout the past year. 

All Members joined in congratulating Cllr Carthy on his performance over the past year with 
many highlighting the outgoing Cathaoirleach’s long list of achievements and contributions 
to the work of the Council during his term of office.

On a vote of 21 votes in favour of Cllr. Murphy and 9 votes in favour of Cllr. Donohoe, 
Cllr. Barbara Anne Murphy was declared elected Cathaoirleach of Wexford County 
Council for the 2021 - 2022 term of office.  

Cllr. Murphy took the Chair. 

The Cathaoirleach thanked her proposer and seconder and commiserated with her 
colleague Cllr. Donohoe. Cllr. Murphy stated that among her priorities for the coming 
year will be Housing, Job Creation and Community Development, and she stated her 
intention to be a Cathaoirleach for all.  

Cllr. Murphy expressed her wish to see the Council progress a range capital projects 
including  Enniscorthy  Technology  Park,  improvements  at  the  Old  Dublin  Road 
Enniscorthy, Trinity Wharf Wexford, St Waleran’s Gorey, New Ross Fire Station and 
developments at Rosslare Port. The Cathaoirleach  also noted that the Council will 
continue to apply for RRDF and URDR funding and indicated her strong support for 
these applications. 

Cllr. Murphy thanked her family for their generous love and support throughout her 
political career and for accepting the personal sacrifices that were part and parcel of 
the  life  of  a  political  family.  She  also  paid  tribute  to  her  late  father  Rory,  a  former 
Cathaoirleach of Wexford County Council and said she was extremely proud to be 
the  third  female  in  the  history of  local government  in Wexford  to  hold  the  office  of 
Cathaoirleach. 

All elected members, together with the Chief Executive joined in congratulating the 
incoming  Cathaoirleach  on  her  election  and wished  her  every success  in  the  year 
ahead.
